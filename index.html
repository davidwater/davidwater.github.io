Hi, my name is Hsiu-Tsu Shui. This website is the place to demonstrate my works and engage with you! If you find anything interesting or resonating, feel free to reach out. I would love to meet you!

I am currently a first-year graduate student in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Michigan Ann Arbor, with a focus on control and navigation systems. My academic journey is driven by a deep interest in the intricacies of autonomous systems and their applications across various domains.

Before joining the University of Michigan, I was a research assistant in the Intelligent Unmanned Aircraft System Laboratory, Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics, National Cheng Kung University, Taiwan. During this time, I engaged in cutting-edge research on unmanned vehicle systems, particularly in the areas of navigation and control. My work primarily revolved around enhancing Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) algorithms and integrating robust strategies to enable real-time multi-UAV operations.

My passion for autonomous systems also led me to contribute to the development of in-lane-level electric vehicles. I specialized in sensor fusion techniques, ensuring precise and reliable data integration for autonomous vehicle applications. Additionally, I gained valuable experience as a research internship at the Mobile Robotics and Autonomous Systems Laboratory at Polytechnique Montréal, where I honed my skills in mobile robotics from September to December 2022.

For more information, please refer to my CV.

Path Planning and Collision Avoidance of Multiple UAV System Based on Particle Swarm Optimization with Kinematic Consideration
We improved traditional Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) through the kinematics-consideration cost function to avoid slow convergence rate and local solutions. In order to verify the feasibility of our method, both simulations and flight experiments were performed.

Software Testing for the Mango System
Designed a software quality assurance plan for the Mango system, which is a machine to machine (M2M) system responsible for gathering and processing the data, then presenting the raw data in a comprehensible way to users.
