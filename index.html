Hi, my name is Hsiu-Tsu Shui. This website is the place to demonstrate my works and engage with you! If you find anything interesting or resonating, feel free to reach out. I would love to meet you!

I am a research assistant in the Intelligent Unmanned Aircraft System Laboratory, Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics, National Cheng Kung University, Taiwan. My research interests broadly cover unmanned vehicle systems, and navigation and control. With my advisor Prof. Ying-Chih Lai, we are developing path planning and obstacle avoidance algorithms for multi-agent systems. Presently, my research is centered on improving Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) with robust strategies that can be further extended to multi-UAV real-time applications. Additionally, I am actively participating in the project regarding the development of a in-lane level electric vehicles. My primary responsibility involves sensor fusions with a specific focus on autonomous vehicle applications.

Previously, I interned at the mobile robotics and autonomous systems laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Jérôme Le Ny while participating in the software cluster of an exchange program at Polytechnique Montréal from September to December 2022.

From fall 2018 to summer 2022, I pursued my major of Bachelor degree in Aeronautics and Astronautics at NCKU. During my academic journey, I gained valuable experiences in the fields of robotics, computer vision, and embedded systems. Moreover, I had the opportunity to work as an intern, focusing on aircraft structural analysis at National Taiwan University.

Based on my previous experience, I have completed several projects (scrolling down this page). Therefore, my interdiciplinary background has strengthened my resolve to pursue a Master or Ph.D. degree in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering, Electrical and Computer Engineering, Robotics Engineering, or other related fields.

Path Planning and Collision Avoidance of Multiple UAV System Based on Particle Swarm Optimization with Kinematic Consideration
We improved traditional Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) through the kinematics-consideration cost function to avoid slow convergence rate and local solutions. In order to verify the feasibility of our method, both simulations and flight experiments were performed.
